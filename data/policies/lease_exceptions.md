# LEASE EXCEPTIONS & SPECIAL APPROVALS

## OVERVIEW

Standard lease terms can be modified with written approval. All exceptions must be:
1. Documented in writing with both parties' signatures
2. Approved by regional director or above
3. Retained in resident file for 3+ years
4. Reviewed during renewal

## COMMON EXCEPTIONS

### Rent Concession
**When approved:** 
- Market conditions (high vacancy rate)
- Resident retention (preventing move-out)
- Lease renewal negotiations
- Early renewal commitment (>6 months before expiration)

**Limits:**
- Maximum discount: 10% of monthly rent
- Duration: One lease term (typically 12 months)
- Not available for non-renewal situations

**Approval:** Regional Director approval required

Example: $1,500 rent → $1,350 (10% discount) for 12 months

### Extended Lease Term
**When approved:**
- Resident requests longer commitment (18-24 months)
- Improves property occupancy stability
- Allows rent concession for extended term

**Limits:**
- Maximum 24-month lease term
- Extends renewal options by proportional amount

**Approval:** Property Manager approval

Example: Standard 12-month lease extended to 18 months with 2% rent discount

### Pet Policy Exception
**When approved:**
- Resident has breed outside policy
- Breed exception for service animals (already exempt)
- Additional pet beyond 2-pet limit (rare)

**Requirements:**
- Enhanced pet deposit (2x normal)
- Pet liability insurance ($300,000 minimum)
- Behavioral assessment by certified evaluator
- Annual re-approval

**Approval:** Regional Director approval required

Example: "Prohibited breed" exception with $600 pet deposit + insurance

### Income Verification Exception
**When approved:**
- Retired resident with investment income
- Self-employed with variable income
- Recent job change

**Requirements:**
- Written explanation of income situation
- Supporting documentation (tax returns, investment statements)
- May require lower debt-to-income ratio
- Personal guarantor may be required

**Approval:** Leasing Manager approval

Example: Recent graduate with parent as income guarantor

### Co-Signer / Guarantor Arrangement
**When approved:**
- Primary applicant income insufficient
- Credit score below minimum
- Rental history weak

**Requirements:**
- Co-signer must have credit score >650
- Co-signer income >2x rent
- Both parties sign lease as equally liable
- Co-signer is full legal liability for rent/damages

**Approval:** Leasing Manager approval

Example: Lease requires parent as co-signer; both responsible for rent

### Accessibility Modifications
**When approved:**
- Resident has documented disability
- Reasonable accommodation under ADA
- Examples: grab bars, ramp, accessible parking, modified fixtures

**Requirements:**
- Written request with disability documentation
- Independent assessment of need
- Not cosmetic or luxury improvement
- Restored to original condition upon move-out (or cost deducted)

**Approval:** No formal approval needed (Legal requirement) but must document

Example: Tenant requires grab bars in bathroom (ADA reasonable accommodation)

### Lease Break Fee Waiver
**When approved:** 
- Military deployment (with military orders)
- Medical emergency (with documentation)
- Domestic violence situation (with protective order)
- Job loss within 30 days of move-in

**Requirements:**
- Documentation provided
- Reasonable timeframe to vacate
- Unit in good condition
- No outstanding rent

**Approval:** Regional Director approval

Example: Active military resident deployed overseas; lease break fee waived with military orders

### Renewal at Below-Market Rent
**When approved:**
- Resident retention strategy
- Long-term good resident
- Market softening
- Competitive move-out risk

**Limits:**
- Can discount up to 5% below market
- Maximum 1 lease term duration
- Cannot increase above market at next renewal

**Approval:** Regional Director approval

Example: Market rent $1,600; renewal offered at $1,500 to retain resident

## EXCEPTION APPROVAL PROCESS

1. **Request:** Property manager submits written request with justification
2. **Documentation:** All supporting documents attached
3. **Cost Analysis:** Impact on rent/revenue calculated
4. **Approval:** Regional Director reviews (or CEO for major exceptions)
5. **Execution:** Exception clause added to lease, signed by both parties
6. **Filing:** Retained in resident file; scan to central system

## EXCEPTION TRACKING & REPORTING

All exceptions tracked in monthly reporting:
- Number of exceptions granted
- Type of exception (concession, extension, etc.)
- Revenue impact
- Approval authority
- Resident name and lease term

Quarterly review for trends: If exceptions >10% of renewals, strategy reviewed.

## RESTRICTIONS ON EXCEPTIONS

**CANNOT Offer Exceptions For:**
- Protected class favoritism (cannot offer rent discount only to certain races)
- Discriminatory accommodations
- Illegal activities
- Below-minimum income requirements without documentation
- Permanent lease term modifications

Any exceptions that appear discriminatory are flagged for legal review.
