# FAIR HOUSING COMPLIANCE GUIDELINES

## FEDERAL LAW OVERVIEW

Portico is committed to compliance with:
- Fair Housing Act (FHA) of 1968
- Civil Rights Act of 1964
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- State and local fair housing laws

## PROTECTED CLASSES

It is ILLEGAL to discriminate based on:
1. Race or color
2. National origin
3. Religion
4. Sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, gender identity)
5. Familial status (families with children under 18)
6. Disability (physical or mental)

Enforcement through HUD, state AG, or private lawsuits with up to 3x damages.

## SCREENING REQUIREMENTS

### What IS Permitted:
- Credit checks (same criteria for all applicants)
- Criminal background checks (must have legitimate business reason)
- Income verification (typically 3x rent rule)
- Employment verification
- Rental history checks

### What is NOT Permitted:
- Higher credit scores for protected classes
- Blanket denials for any criminal conviction (must consider nature, severity, recency)
- Denying families with children
- Denying applicants with disabilities
- Different application fees by family status or disability

## REASONABLE ACCOMMODATIONS

Residents with disabilities have right to request reasonable accommodations, such as:
- Accessible parking spaces
- Service animal housing (no pet fees)
- Accessible entrances/elevators
- Accessible unit modifications

All requests must be documented and responded to within 10 business days.

## FAMILIAL STATUS PROTECTION

- Cannot refuse to rent to families with children
- Cannot charge higher rent/deposits for families
- Cannot restrict children from common areas
- Cannot require different lease terms

Violators face up to $16,000+ in HUD penalties.

## ADVERTISING

Fair Housing Act requires ads to state "Equal Housing Opportunity." Avoid language that suggests discrimination:
- "No children" ❌
- "Only professionals" ❌
- "Good credit required" (unless clear criteria) ❌

## ENFORCEMENT & REPORTING

Violations must be reported to:
- HUD Office: 1-800-669-9777
- State Attorney General
- Community legal aid organizations

Staff training: Annual fair housing compliance training required for all leasing and management staff.
