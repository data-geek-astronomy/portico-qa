# ACCESSIBILITY & ADA COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

## LEGAL FRAMEWORK

Portico must comply with:
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990
- Fair Housing Act (FHA) amendments (1988)
- Rehabilitation Act Section 504
- State accessibility laws

Applies to all 50 communities regardless of size. Penalties for non-compliance: up to $75,000+ per violation.

## REASONABLE ACCOMMODATIONS

Residents with disabilities have right to request modifications, such as:
- Grab bars in bathroom
- Accessible parking space (closer to unit)
- Accessible entrance (ramp, level entry)
- Modified light switches/door hardware
- Accessible appliances
- Service animal housing (exemption from pet fees)
- Emotional support animal accommodations

**Process:**
1. Resident submits written request to property manager
2. Documentation required: Letter from healthcare provider or therapist
3. Management reviews within 10 business days
4. If reasonable: Approved and scheduled
5. If unreasonable: Denial with written explanation

**Approval Timeframe:** Response required within 10 days (approval or denial with reason)

Example denial: Resident requests hot tub installation (luxury, not necessity)

## SERVICE ANIMALS VS. EMOTIONAL SUPPORT ANIMALS

**Service Animals (FHA Covered):**
- Trained to perform specific disability-related tasks
- Can be any dog breed or size
- Exempt from pet fees and breed restrictions
- No disability documentation required (observable task performance)
- Can accompany resident in common areas

Examples: Guide dogs, seizure alert dogs, mobility assistance dogs

**Emotional Support Animals (Requires Verification):**
- Not trained for specific tasks
- Provide comfort through companionship
- Requires documentation from licensed mental health professional
- Still subject to pet fees (but no breed restrictions)
- Cannot be excluded for breed

Required documentation: Letter from therapist/psychiatrist stating:
- Resident's disability (e.g., anxiety, depression, PTSD)
- Necessity of animal for disability relief
- Licensed provider's contact info

**Fraud Prevention:** 
- Many online "ESA certificate" vendors are fraudulent
- Portico verifies provider credentials before accepting documentation
- May require additional verification if provider suspect

## ACCESSIBLE UNITS

Properties must provide:
- 5% of units fully ADA-compliant (ground floor, wider doorways, accessible bathroom)
- Parking spaces: 1 per 25 units accessible, 1 per 25 accessible van space
- Common areas accessible by ramp or elevator
- Accessible restrooms in common areas
- Accessible signage with Braille

## WEBSITE & DIGITAL ACCESSIBILITY

Portico website and online portal must comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA:
- Keyboard navigation
- Screen reader compatibility
- High contrast text
- Descriptive alt-text for images
- Captions on videos

Non-compliance can result in lawsuits and ADA fines.

## COMMUNICATION ACCESSIBILITY

Must provide accommodations for residents with hearing/vision disabilities:
- TTY phone (text telephone) for lease inquiries
- Email as alternative to phone communication
- Large print lease documents (at least 14-point font)
- Audio documents for vision-impaired residents
- ASL interpreter availability for lease signings

Costs borne by Portico.

## MODIFICATION & RESTORATION

**Who pays for modifications?**

**Portico pays for:**
- Ramp installation (accessibility requirement)
- Grab bar installation (bathroom safety)
- Door hardware modifications (accessibility)
- Accessible parking designation (paint/signage)

**Resident may pay for:**
- Cosmetic upgrades (if approved and resident desires)
- Modifications beyond reasonable accommodation scope

**Upon move-out:**
- Accessibility features stay (required by law)
- Cannot charge resident for fixture removal
- Example: Cannot deduct grab bar removal cost from deposit

## DISABILITY DISCLOSURE

**What Portico Can Ask:**
- "Do you need any reasonable accommodations?"
- "Is there anything we can do to make this unit/community accessible?"

**What Portico CANNOT Ask:**
- "Do you have a disability?" (unless already disclosed)
- "What is your disability?" (unless directly related to need)
- Request medical records or diagnosis details
- Ask for disability documentation unless requesting service/ESA animal

## COMPLAINT & ENFORCEMENT

Violations reported to:
- HUD Office: 1-800-669-9777
- Department of Justice Civil Rights Division
- State Attorney General
- Private lawsuits under ADA

Damages can include:
- Actual damages (often substantial)
- Up to 3x damages for willful violations
- Attorney's fees
- Injunctive relief (forced modifications)

**Case examples:**
- Resident denied service dog access: $10,000+ settlement
- Inaccessible parking: $5,000-15,000 settlement
- Website ADA violation: $15,000+ settlement

## STAFF TRAINING

All staff must receive annual ADA/FHA training:
- Property managers
- Leasing agents
- Maintenance staff
- Office staff

Training covers:
- Legal requirements
- Reasonable accommodations process
- Service vs. emotional support animals
- Communication accessibility
- Disability etiquette

Training documentation kept for 3 years.
