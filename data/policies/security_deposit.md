# SECURITY DEPOSIT POLICY

## DEPOSIT AMOUNT

Standard deposit equals one month's rent. Example: $1,500/month rent = $1,500 deposit

Higher deposits may be charged for:
- Residents with less than 2-year rental history
- Credit score <600
- History of eviction or lease violation
- Pets (additional pet deposit may apply)

Maximum deposit per state law: 1-2 months' rent (varies by state)

## DEPOSIT USES

Deposits held in separate escrow account and used ONLY for:
1. Unpaid rent (including holdover rent)
2. Damages beyond normal wear and tear
3. Lease violation costs (pest treatment, unauthorized repairs)
4. Cleaning costs if unit not returned in clean condition

Deposits CANNOT be used for:
- Landlord's normal maintenance
- Rent increases
- Attorney's fees for non-payment
- Cosmetic damage from normal use

## NORMAL WEAR & TEAR

**Examples of normal wear (NOT deductible):**
- Light scuff marks on walls
- Small nail holes from pictures
- Worn carpet in high-traffic areas
- Faded paint from sun exposure
- Minor paint touch-ups

**Examples of damage (DEDUCTIBLE):**
- Large holes punched in walls
- Stains or burns on carpet
- Broken windows
- Missing doors/fixtures
- Water damage from negligence
- Pet damage (urine, chewing, scratching)
- Appliance damage from misuse

## DEPOSIT RETURN TIMELINE

**Within 30 days of move-out:**
1. Final inspection completed
2. Damage assessed and quoted
3. Itemized deduction list prepared
4. Remaining deposit mailed to resident

**Itemized list must include:**
- Damage description
- Repair cost estimate (from vendor quote)
- Deductions for rent owed, utilities, cleaning

**If no deductions:**
- Full deposit refunded within 30 days
- No itemization needed

## DEDUCTION AMOUNTS

Standard market costs by item:
- Carpet cleaning: $100-150 per room
- Carpet replacement: $400-800 per room
- Paint touch-up: $50-100 per wall
- Paint full room: $150-300
- Appliance repair: $75-200
- Appliance replacement: $300-800
- Plumbing repair: $75-150
- Pest treatment: $150-250
- Deep cleaning: $150-300 (if needed)

All costs must be supported by vendor quotes or invoices.

## INTEREST ON DEPOSITS

Per state law requirements (varies):
- California: Requires interest paid on deposits held >1 year
- Texas: No interest required
- New York: Interest in non-interest bearing account
- Most states: No interest required

Portico policy: Deposits held in non-interest bearing escrow; check local law.

## DISPUTES OVER DEDUCTIONS

If resident disputes deductions:
1. Send written explanation within 7 days of receiving list
2. Management reviews with property manager
3. If disputed amount >$200: obtain independent estimate
4. Resolve within 14 days

If cannot resolve: May pursue small claims court (deposit claim limit varies by state).

## LEASE BREAK FEES VS. DEPOSITS

If resident breaks lease early:
- Lease break fee charged separately (not from deposit)
- Example: 1.5 months' rent lease break fee
- Deposit still held separately for move-out damage

Resident liable for: Lease break fee + Deposits (both separate)

## SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES

**Death of resident:**
- Deposit treated same as normal move-out
- Process with estate executor or next of kin
- Return within 30 days to probate court or designated party

**Eviction:**
- If judgment entered against resident
- Deposit applied to judgment amount first
- Any remaining judgment pursued through collection

**Habitability Issues:**
- If unit uninhabitable during lease
- Resident may withhold rent and repair costs
- Cannot withhold from deposit (separate matter)
