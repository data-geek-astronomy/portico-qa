# EVICTION PROCEDURES & LEASE TERMINATION

## GROUNDS FOR LEASE TERMINATION

**Non-Curable Violations (Immediate 3-Day Notice):**
- Manufacturing/possession of drugs
- Violence or threats of violence
- Operating illegal business from unit
- Selling/distributing controlled substances

**Curable Violations (14-Day Notice to Cure):**
- Non-payment of rent
- Unauthorized occupants
- Subletting without consent
- Lease violations (pets, noise, etc.)
- Property damage beyond normal wear

**At-Fault Non-Renewal:**
- Lease violations after warning
- Failure to cure within 14 days
- Multiple violations in 12 months

## NOTICE PROCEDURES

### Notice to Pay or Quit (Non-Payment)
1. Three (3) days written notice delivered (email + certified mail)
2. Notice includes: amount owed, due date, late fees
3. If rent paid within 3 days: process stops
4. If not paid: proceed to eviction filing

### Notice to Cure or Quit (Lease Violations)
1. Fourteen (14) days written notice
2. Notice specifies violation and required action
3. If cured within 14 days: process stops
4. If not cured: proceed to eviction filing

### Unconditional Quit (Non-Curable)
1. Three (3) days written notice (or per state law)
2. Delivered in person if possible, certified mail as backup
3. No opportunity to cure
4. Proceed to eviction filing immediately

## LEGAL EVICTION PROCESS

1. **Filing:** File complaint in District Court
   - Include: lease terms, violation details, notice proof
   - Pay court filing fees ($150-300 typical)
   
2. **Service:** Serve resident with court papers
   - Must be done by sheriff or process server
   - Proof of service filed with court
   
3. **Court Date:** Hearing before judge (typically 7-14 days)
   - Portico presents evidence: lease, notices, rent ledger
   - Resident has right to appear and defend
   - Judge issues judgment (eviction or dismissal)
   
4. **Judgment:** If landlord wins
   - Writ of Restitution issued
   - 5-day appeal period
   - If not appealed: Sheriff removes resident
   
5. **Removal:** Sheriff executes removal
   - Resident must vacate by specified date/time
   - Personal belongings may be removed by resident before removal
   - Sheriff changes locks after removal

## DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

**For Every Violation:**
- Incident report (date, time, description, witness info)
- Photos of damage (if applicable)
- Maintenance logs
- Rent payment history
- Copy of lease
- All notices delivered (with proof of delivery)

Documentation kept for 3+ years for legal defense.

## HOLDOVER RENT

If resident fails to vacate after lease expiration:
- Rent increases to 150% of monthly rent per day
- Resident becomes month-to-month tenant
- Only 30-day notice to vacate required
- Example: $1,500 rent = $2,250/day holdover charge

## FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY

Eviction costs borne by Portico:
- Court filing fees: $150-300
- Sheriff service: $150-200
- Attorney fees: $300-800+ (if contested)
- Lost rent during legal process: varies

If resident is judgment debtor, can attempt collection through:
- Small claims (if <$10,000)
- Collection agency
- Wage garnishment
- Bank account levy

## STATE-SPECIFIC VARIATIONS

Portico operates in multiple states with different eviction laws:
- Notice periods may vary (3-30 days per state)
- Court procedures vary
- Tenant protections vary
- Always consult with local counsel before filing

Examples:
- California: 30-day notice, strong tenant protections
- Texas: 3-day notice, faster process
- New York: 14-30 day notice, strong tenant rights
